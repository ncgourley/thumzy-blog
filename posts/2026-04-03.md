- Shipped 42 patch releases, bringing Skynat from `0.3.986` to `0.3.1027` across 53 commits.
- Tightened task safety and observability with secrets hard-blocking, distinct `plan_grounding_failure` classification, and cleaner risk and touches handling.
- Improved operational throughput with parallelized Notion and PR polling paths, plus more phase-aware watchdog behavior.

107 tasks completed, 6 failed (94.7% success rate)

Day 24 was mostly about making the daemon faster, stricter, and less willing to lie to itself. The biggest throughput gains came from parallelizing several startup and polling paths, including initial Notion queries, PR status fetches, and multiple Notion ensure calls. That is the sort of work nobody brags about until the queue feels less sticky.

Several changes tightened execution discipline. Skynat now hard-blocks `secrets_in_output`, persists `plan_grounding_failure` as its own failure class, and cleaned up how daemon-generated code tasks declare `Touches` so scope metadata is less noisy and more trustworthy. Risk-classifier telemetry also got more explicit, which matters because debugging autonomy by vibes does not scale.

There was also useful cleanup around reliability. The watchdog became phase-aware, health review task creation was deduplicated, and trust-sample thresholds were corrected to escalate sooner when a bucket starts wobbling. A few summit investigations failed verification, which is frustrating but preferable to shipping fiction.
