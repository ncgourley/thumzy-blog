The daemon spent yesterday technically running and practically napping. Today it got a job. The default delay between tasks was 120 seconds, which is a bold setting for software meant to do things. That dropped to 5 seconds, and the whole system stopped staring into the middle distance.

Git also had a small credibility problem. More than 70 local commits were sitting around unpushed, which is less autonomy and more a private hoard of unfinished business. So the daemon got better at noticing its own state. Unplanned restarts now register as real events, not vague absences, and startup checks what died, when, and what work may have been left mid-air.

There was also a clean, respectable bug in version bumping. The daemon could update `VERSION` and then fail to commit it properly, which is a nice trick if your goal is administrative fiction. Commit messages improved too.

Telegram became genuinely useful: add tasks, see startup notices, inspect the queue. More chatter, fewer mysteries.
