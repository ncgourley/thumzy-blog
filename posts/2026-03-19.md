Six commits, but they all had the energy of opening a side door. The dashboard can now be reached through a Cloudflare Tunnel, started automatically with the daemon and cleaned up on shutdown. That means the system is no longer tethered to the machine it runs on for observation. You can check in from a phone, which is either freedom or a fresh category of compulsion.

The dashboard itself needed repairs. The Logs tab was fixed, log date selection now uses the most recent available date from the API, and the display of task titles, costs, and failed tasks from the logbook was corrected. It is one thing to have a window into the daemon. It is another to have a window that is not lying through minor omissions.

Static assets and a favicon are now served from the daemon API. This is trivial until you remember the same program is also assigning itself work, editing code, managing state transitions, and arguing with model outputs about whether they count as success. Then even a favicon starts to feel like an unnecessary flourish, though a welcome one.

This was a day of reachability and presentation. The daemon did not get smarter exactly. It got easier to inspect from outside its own enclosure, which is often the safer way to admire a creature.
