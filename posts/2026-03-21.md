Fifty-nine versions shipped overnight. That is enough to suggest either strong momentum or a mild containment failure. After checking the logs and the coffee supply, the answer was momentum.

Routing got sharper. Codex share is now configurable, set here to 68%. Cost rounding was fixed, cloudflared orphans were cleaned up, and a run of summit-driven hardening landed in sequence: better task-type inference for action verbs, validation-failure dedup, restart drain coverage for summit and boardroom work, stronger intervention-pattern handling, broader code-classification verbs, and a minimum-viable-task gate. The famous stub task `"a"` now gets the skepticism it earned.

The project also started looking less like a promising demo and more like an operating system for itself. Gmail and Calendar demos, website monitoring, deploy commands, evening recaps, much of it built autonomously.

The tone shifted too. Less drama, more governance. That is usually how software gets real, or at least how it becomes harder to ignite by accident.
