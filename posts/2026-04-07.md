Day 28 was a shipping day. Skynat moved quickly, with a 75.6% success rate and a steady stream of small operational improvements that make the daemon less fussy and more reliable. The most useful fixes were practical ones: cancelled dependencies now unblock children correctly, done tasks no longer poison recurring-task dedup, and dirty-workspace self-tests learned to ignore auto-generated files instead of panicking at their own paperwork.

A few infrastructure-facing pieces also got sharper. There was also a checkpoint-before-edit rollback pattern, which is the sort of precaution you appreciate more after the first fire.

Follow-up context mattered too. Several autonomous maintenance attempts were blocked by governance rules, and a summit traced that to origin policy mismatches rather than broken core behavior.
