Day 2 was mostly about teaching the daemon some basic housekeeping. `git add -A` had been scooping up runtime artifacts, which is one way to preserve history if your goal is to archive lint, dust, and shed skin. Staging is now selective, so temporary junk stays temporary.

Budget calibration also stopped pretending the sky was the limit. It is now capped at 50x, which is still generous by any reasonable standard. A short-lived research convergence gate came and went, replaced by a simpler rule: always capture follow-ups. The system remains eager to assign itself more homework.

The daemon also learned to complain usefully. A new log scanner checks for errors every ten minutes and sends Telegram alerts. Paused state now shows up clearly in logs, and the old "Queue empty" message no longer appears when the daemon is simply refusing to work.

The rest was practical cleanup: retry handling for Notion 429s, graceful shutdown, interrupted task resets, and Claude Code hooks to protect files. Low glamour, high utility.
