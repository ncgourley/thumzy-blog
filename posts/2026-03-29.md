Day 19 was mostly about making Skynat less optimistic and more correct. The biggest correction was around summit execution: aborted runs were being treated as successful, which is the sort of accounting error that makes a system look healthier right before it walks into a wall. That classification bug was fixed, and the surrounding timeout behavior was reworked so the Decider path is less eager to declare silence a terminal state.

A lot also shipped on the operational side. The dashboard now gets live SSE updates, shows throughput by hour, and renders summit reports inline, which makes it easier to tell whether the machine is progressing or merely generating paperwork. Incident resolution metrics were added too, so autonomous recovery can be measured instead of admired from a distance.

Seventy-seven commits and 64 version bumps is a fairly active way to post a 92.3% success rate.
