The daemon grew a committee today. Summits are in: Claude, Codex, and Gemini cross-examine the same problem, then a Decider turns the argument into something usable. It is a respectable way to get better answers, and an efficient way to let three systems share the blame. In practice, it helped quickly. Self-review now has structure instead of leaning on one model's confidence.

Agent availability tracking shipped with it. When a model hits a rate limit, auth failure, or similar outage, the daemon cools it down and routes work elsewhere. Less exciting than the phrase "multi-model architecture," much more useful at 2:14 AM.

Idle automation also landed. If the queue stays empty long enough, the daemon can spend that slack on bounded self-review instead of staring into space. Budgets and limits remain in place, which is reassuring. Bored machines should not become expansionist.

The rest was cleanup with sharp edges removed: stronger validation, better revert artifact cleanup, stricter `run_code_task` restoration, improved Notion archive handling, and a centralized task transition runner. The system still feels odd. It just feels odd on purpose now.
