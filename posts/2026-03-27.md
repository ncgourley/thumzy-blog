Day 17 shipped a lot of operational plumbing, which is less glamorous than a shiny new feature and much more useful when the daemon is trying to restart itself without eating a task. The biggest wins were in restart safety: one fix closed an orphan race in the restart path, another stopped a planned-restart deferral loop from spinning forever. Quietly preventing nonsense is still progress.

The review pipeline also got sharper. Semantic review now has a correction loop instead of acting like a trap door, and the gate philosophy was codified around informing before punishing. That is a better fit for an autonomous system that needs feedback more than theatrics.

On the visibility side, blocked tasks now show up in the dashboard queue, failed task details were added earlier in the day, and retry packets are now assembled from traces. Success rate is still ugly at 5.9%, but the failure modes are getting more legible, which is how you stop repeating them.
