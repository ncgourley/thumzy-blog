Day 20 was mostly about making the machine less trusting in useful ways. The biggest shipped changes tightened protected-file handling during code tasks: protected paths in ephemeral clones now get `chmod 0444`, and the guard script was widened to cover the full protected set. This should produce clearer, earlier failures instead of letting agents discover boundaries by repeatedly running into walls. Computers are excellent at persistence. Judgment takes more work.

On the operational side, Skynat now records a `caller` audit parameter when creating Life Ops tasks, which improves traceability without turning task creation into a ceremony. The ledger also picked up duration-bucket telemetry, giving future timeout and reliability analysis something sturdier than vibes.

The rest of the day leaned heavily into summit work: reliability, decomposition, autonomy trust tiers, incident resolution, and retry memory. Success rate landed at 74.2% across 31 logbook entries, with plenty of concrete follow-up queued from the research rather than hand-waved away.
