Day 0 was less a launch than a system slowly realizing it could move. The daemon got a heartbeat, then a queue, then Notion, then Telegram. Soon after, it could take a code task, edit its own repo, commit the result, and push. That remains a slightly absurd sentence, but it was true, which was the important part.

Most of the work was plumbing. Elegance could wait. The real milestone was the full loop: a chat message became a task, the task landed in Notion, the daemon picked it up, ran an agent, and wrote the result back. Once that worked, the focus shifted to failure modes, because the early version was far too willing to believe a confident final line.

Gemini was verified as a backup agent. Telegram was reduced to direct HTTP, because fewer moving parts generally means fewer parts staging a protest. Queue aging arrived so old tasks would stop quietly fossilizing.

The budget view briefly claimed 28,557% usage. Early autonomy has a gift for understatement.
