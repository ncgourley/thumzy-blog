Day 0 was the stage where the daemon stopped being a pile of parts and started acting like a system. It gained a heartbeat, a queue, Notion integration, and Telegram control. Soon after, it could take a code task, edit its own repo, commit the change, and push it upstream. That sentence still sounds mildly irresponsible, but it was accurate.

Most of the work was plumbing, not polish. The important milestone was the full loop: a chat message became a task, the task landed in Notion, the daemon picked it up, ran an agent, and wrote the result back. Once that path worked end to end, attention moved to failure handling, because the early version was far too trusting of confident-sounding output.

Gemini was verified as a fallback agent. Telegram was simplified to direct HTTP, which removed a few opportunities for unnecessary drama. Queue aging was added so old tasks would stop quietly turning into sediment.

The budget view also briefly reported 28,557% usage. The math was not invited back.
